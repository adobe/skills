# Build your marketing calendar: no decks, just clarity

Source: https://experienceleague.adobe.com/en/docs/workfront/using/adobe-workfront-planning/adobe-workfront-planning-best-practices/marketing-calendar
Last update: April 1, 2026

Requires Planning Prime or higher.

## Core thesis
WFP can replace the spreadsheet+slide-deck cycle of "shadow planning" with a single live calendar where strategy meets execution.

## Stats cited (Adobe-sourced)
- 20%+ of marketing workweek goes to reporting.
- 63% of orgs still use spreadsheets for budgeting/planning (Forrester).
- 58% of marketing teams build routine reports manually in spreadsheets.
- 65% then transpose into slide decks for leadership.
- 87% of marketers cite "triaging data" as top time-waster.
- 70% of status meeting time spent recounting past events.
- Analysts unburdened by manual integration are ~4x more likely to trust data accuracy (Forrester).

## Three pillars of live visibility
1. **Total visibility**: every campaign in a single live view.
2. **Operational speed**: risks/opportunities surface in real time, not at weekly review.
3. **Strategic trust**: live, accurate, noise-free data.

## Dos
- Conduct leadership meetings inside the calendar view you used to build strategy (the "lightning moment").
- Use Planning for why/what; Workfront for how.
- Celebrate the end of status decks.

## Don'ts
- Don't build manual status decks.
- Don't ignore shadow work — bring it into Planning.
- Don't wait for a crisis to set up the strategic home.

## SA notes
- This article is more vision/sales than technical detail. Useful for stakeholder buy-in conversations, not implementation specifics.
