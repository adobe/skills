# A short ask, for a few designers we trust

Stardust is a tool that redesigns existing websites. Today, the redesigns it
produces share two weaknesses we want your help fixing:

1. **Within a single brand**, the proposals it generates tend to share a
   skeleton and differ mostly on tokens (palette, spacing). They feel like
   variants of one design, not different designs.
2. **Across different brands**, the same is true a level up: a children's
   product and a fintech end up structurally similar, just repainted.

We think both failures are structural. The fix isn't another generator — it's
a system that can **learn from designers** what's stunning, what's competent,
and what's slop, and then keep evolving as designers contribute.

Before we build any of it, we want a small group of designers we trust to
read a 1-page design sketch and tell us where it's wrong, naive, or missing
something obvious.

## What we'd love from you

About **15–30 minutes**, in any form that's natural to you (notes, voice memo,
scribbled-on PDF, in-line comments, a 10-minute call). Specifically:

- An overall verdict: *worth building*, *needs rethinking*, or *no, here's why*.
- Reactions to the **8 open questions** at the bottom of the doc — these are
  the decisions we don't want to make alone.
- Anything that made you wince. Especially things that read like they were
  written by someone who hasn't sat with real design work.

You don't need to read every line. Skim §1 and §4, then go to §8 (the open
questions). The rest is supporting context.

## What this is NOT

- **Not an implementation plan.** No code yet. We deliberately want your read
  before we touch the tool.
- **Not a finished system.** The doc is v0, on purpose. The vocabulary, the
  schema, the verdict levels — all of it is up for revision.
- **Not a request for production-grade exemplars.** We're testing whether the
  *system* makes sense, not seeding the corpus.

## How to respond

Whatever's lowest-friction for you. Reply in email, drop notes in a doc,
record a voice memo, or grab time on the calendar. We'll consolidate
everything and circulate a v1 of the doc that reflects what we heard.

Thank you for your time. The whole point of this system is that it gets
better the more it learns from people who actually know what stunning looks
like — so your read is the load-bearing piece.
