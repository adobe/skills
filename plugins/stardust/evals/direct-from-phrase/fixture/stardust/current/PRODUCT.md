# Product

## Register

brand

<!-- _provenance: inferred — landing-page indicators dominate: marketing hero, social proof strip, pricing page, demo CTA above the fold, no auth wall. -->

## Users

Small-business owners, in-house controllers, and CPAs at accounting
firms. The captured copy addresses a professional finance audience
("controllers", "CPA", "month-end close", "GAAP") in formal
third-person language. Secondary audience: accounting firms managing
client books at scale (the "Firm" pricing tier).

## Product Purpose

Ledgerline is double-entry bookkeeping software for growing
businesses: automatic bank reconciliation, audit-ready reporting, and
accountant collaboration in one place. Success, as the site frames
it, is a faster and more trustworthy month-end close ("We cut our
month-end close from nine days to four").

## Brand Personality

Reserved, precise, institutional. Three words: dependable, exact,
understated. The voice is professional-formal — no contractions,
compliance vocabulary, feature-led headings. Emotional goal: earn the
trust of a skeptical accountant. Nothing on the captured surface
plays for delight or personality; credibility is the whole register.

## Anti-references

<!-- _provenance: inferred — no anti-references are stated on the site; derived from what the design consistently avoids. -->

The current site visibly avoids consumer-fintech flash: no gradients,
no illustration systems, no dark mode, no animated hero. It reads as
the opposite of playful neobank marketing (Cash App, Step) and closer
to legacy accounting-vendor conservatism.

## Design Principles

<!-- _provenance: inferred — derived from consistent observed treatment across all 5 captured pages. -->

- **Trust before flair.** Navy, serif headings, and restrained
  spacing signal institutional reliability on every page.
- **One conversion verb.** "Request a demo" is the dominant CTA
  site-wide (11 instances across 5 pages).
- **Evidence over adjectives.** Copy leads with numbers (4,200
  finance teams, 11,000 institutions, 40 entities).
- **Uniform page anatomy.** Every marketing page repeats the same
  spine: page head, alternating white/mist sections, navy CTA band,
  mega-footer.
